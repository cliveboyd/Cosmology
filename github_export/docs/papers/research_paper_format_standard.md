# Research paper format standard

## Design authority

The user-edited A4 edition of `primordial_abundance_lithium_nucleogenesis_addendum_2026-07-17.docx` is the design authority for future technical research papers.

## Page and language

- Use A4 portrait pages, 210 x 297 mm, with 25.4 mm margins.
- Use UK English spelling and `en-GB` language metadata.
- Keep the title page unnumbered.
- Use the centred running research-paper header on every body page.
- Use a right-aligned `Page N` footer from page 2 onwards.

## Typography and hierarchy

- Preserve the Calibri body type and restrained blue heading hierarchy of the design authority.
- Use short proposition-level paragraphs and deliberate white space rather than compressed pagination.
- Start major sections on a new page when this clarifies the argument, but never create an empty page.
- Keep each heading with at least one substantive paragraph, table, figure or equation block.

## Equations, tables and figures

- Use Cambria Math for equations.
- Align equals signs vertically in grouped equation definitions.
- Fit full-width tables and figures to the 159.2 mm A4 text measure.
- Preserve proportional table columns and figure geometry rather than reducing body type.
- Split tables only at row boundaries, repeat dark-blue header rows, and retain captions with the first table segment.
- Keep callouts generous and full-width for claim boundaries, interpretive distinctions and stop/go decisions.

## Release gate

- Render the DOCX to PDF and inspect every page before release.
- Reject clipping, overlap, blank pages, split headings, isolated captions, broken tables, split callouts and broken equation groups.
- Confirm complete running headers, `Page N` footers, A4 geometry and vertically aligned equation equals signs.
- Archive the citation-facing PDF and update its SHA-256 manifest entry.
