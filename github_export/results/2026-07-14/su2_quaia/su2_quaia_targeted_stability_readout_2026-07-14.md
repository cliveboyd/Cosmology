# SU2 / Quaia Targeted Stability Readout

Date: July 14, 2026

## Result

The targeted nested random-sky mock audit is complete for the two follow-up redshift windows.

| target | observed SNR max | direction spread | p(SNR >= obs) | p(spread <= obs) | joint p |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| broad 0.95-1.45 | 4.785 | 13.47 deg | 0.000666 | 0.007995 | 0.000666 |
| narrow 1.15-1.35 | 3.297 | 18.51 deg | 0.043971 | 0.016656 | 0.005330 |

## Interpretation

The broad `z=0.95-1.45` window is the stronger targeted result: random skies rarely reproduce both its high latitude-cut SNR and its tight directional clustering. The narrow `z=1.15-1.35` window is weaker, but still shows nontrivial coherence in the joint test.

This should be treated as targeted follow-up evidence, not a headline SU2 angular-symmetry claim. The earlier full global look-elsewhere audit did not pass, so this result motivates stricter external-template controls and a pre-registered follow-up scan rather than promotion to discovery language.
